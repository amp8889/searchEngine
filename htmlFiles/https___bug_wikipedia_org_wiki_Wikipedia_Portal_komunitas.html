Pendapat - opinion by Mimihitam[sunting sumber]

Permisi, excuse me, pardon, kulo nuwun. Aga kareba!

Saya lihat di perubahan terbaru Wikipedia Bahasa Bugis, banyak sekali dibuat artikel tentang komune di Perancis. Saya senang bahwa wikipedia bahasa Bugis kembali bergairah, namun saya hendak sedikit mengungkapkan keganjalan di hati saya. Artikel-artikel komune yang sangat banyak itu, sebagian besar hanya satu kalimat yang berisi "Komune x terletak di x, Perancis". Masalahnya adalah, pentingkah? Apakah penting membuat ribuan artikel seperti itu, yang tidak ada manfaatnya sama sekali? Apa yang bisa didapat dari seonggok kalimat itu? Satu artikel bermutu yang lengkap jauh lebih berguna daripada satu juta artikel semacam itu. Menurut saya, untuk wikipedia yang baru akan merangkak seperti Bugis ini, sebaiknya bertumpu pada pembinaan artikel-artikel dasar dalam berbagai bidang terlebih dahulu. Misalnya: proton, oksigen, tata surya, dan lain semacamnya. Selanjutnya artikel-artikel dasar itu dilengkapi, atau paling tidak, bisa dibaca dan dipetik manfaatnya dari para pembaca. Manfaatnya akan jauh lebih terasa, daripada jutaan artikel tak bermanfaat seperti itu. Ini hanyalah seonggok pendapat dari saya, yang merasa sedikit prihatin dengan kejar-kejaran jumlah artikel, tanpa memedulikan mutu. Kita harus bertumpu pada mutu, bukan kuantitas, karena ini ensiklopedia. Tingkatkan mutu artikel-artikel dasar, dan saya yakin Wikipedia Bugis akan maju. Terima kasih dan salam persaudaraan!

I have seen that in the recent change of Bugis Wikipedia, there are so many articles about commune in France. I am happy that Bugis Wikipedia has returned to the stage! However, i want to express my concern. These articles, most of them only consist of, "Commune x is located in xx, France". The problem is, is it important? Is it important to create thousands of articles like that, which dont contain any benefit? What can we get from the one-sentence thing? One good, or even best article, about a topic, is far more useful than one million one-sentence commune article. I think, for new wikipedia like Bugis, the wiki should focus on the creation of basic articles, for example proton, oxygen, solar system, etc. Next, the basic articles must be comprehensive, or at least, readable, and the benefit can be picked by the reader. It will be far more useful, rather than a million meaningless commune article. No offence, this is just my opinion, who is concerned with total article rank race like this. We should focus on the quality, not quantity, because this is encyclopedia. Increase the quality of basic articles, and i am sure that Bugis Wikipedia will be a great wiki. Thank you for your attention! Mimihitam 10:45, 2 Februari 2011 (UTC)m.

[Objective] The objective for the new skin is to make the interface more welcoming and comfortable for readers and useful for advanced users. It draws inspiration from previous requests, the Community Wishlist Surveys, and gadgets and scripts. The work helped our code follow the standards and improve all other skins. We reduced PHP code in Wikimedia deployed skins by 75%. The project has also focused on making it easier to support gadgets and use APIs.

  • The sticky header makes it easier to find tools that editors use often. It decreases scrolling to the top of the page by 16%.
  • The new table of contents makes it easier to navigate to different sections. Readers and editors jumped to different sections of the page 50% more than with the old table of contents. It also looks a bit different on talk pages.
  • The new search bar is easier to find and makes it easier to find the correct search result from the list. This increased the amount of searches started by 30% on the wikis we tested on.
  • The skin does not negatively affect pageviews, edit rates, or account creation. There is evidence of increases in pageviews and account creation across partner communities.

This wiki will soon be able to use the inline audio player implemented by the Phonos extension. This is part of fulfilling a wishlist proposal of providing audio links that play on click.

With the inline audio player, you can add text-to-speech audio snippets to wiki pages by simply using a tag: 

<phonos file="audio file" label="Listen"/>

The above tag will show the text next to a speaker icon, and clicking on it will play the audio instantly without taking you to another page. A common example where you can use this feature is in adding pronunciation to words as illustrated on the English Wiktionary below. 

{{audio|en|En-uk-English.oga|Audio (UK)}}

<phonos file="En-uk-English.oga" label="Audio (UK)"/>
