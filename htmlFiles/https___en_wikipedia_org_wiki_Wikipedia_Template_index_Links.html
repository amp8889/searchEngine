Category[edit]

What to type What it makes Where it goes
{{cat main}}
links talk edit
The main article for this category is Template index/Links.
 Category near top, for when an article exists that has the same name as the category.
{{cat main|articlename}}
links talk edit
The main article for this category is articlename.
 Category near top, for when the main article about a category is not the same as the category name.
{{cat more|articlename}}
links talk edit
For more information, see articlename.
 Like cat main, but with alternate wording.

German titles[edit]

What to type What it makes Where it goes
{{German title Freiherr}}
links talk edit 		Regarding personal names: Freiherr is a former title (translated as Baron). In Germany since 1919, it forms part of family names. The feminine forms are Freifrau and Freiin. Article
near bottom
(use when Freiherr (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title Fürst}}
links talk edit 		Regarding personal names: Fürst is a title, translated as Prince, not a first or middle name. The feminine form is Fürstin. Article
near bottom
(use when Fürst (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title Graf}}
links talk edit 		Regarding personal names: Until 1919, Graf was a title, translated as Count, not a first or middle name. The female form is Gräfin. In Germany, it has formed part of family names since 1919. Article
near bottom
(use when Graf (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title Herzog}}
links talk edit 		Regarding personal names: Herzog is a title, translated as Duke, not a first or middle name. The female form is Herzogin. Article
near bottom
(use when Herzog (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title Prinz}}
links talk edit 		Regarding personal names: Prinz is a title, translated as Prince, not a first or middle name. The female form is Prinzessin. Article
near bottom
(use when Prinz (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title Reichsfreiherr}}
links talk edit 		Reichsfreiherr is a German title of nobility, usually translated as Baron of the Empire. Freiherr is a title usually translated as Baron, and Reich is usually translated as Empire. The female forms are Reichsfreifrau and Reichsfreiin. Titles using the prefix Reichs- were not created after the fall of the Holy Roman Empire. Article
near bottom
(use when Reichsfreiherr (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title Ritter}}
links talk edit 		Regarding personal names: Ritter is a title, translated approximately as Sir (denoting a Knight), not a first or middle name. There is no equivalent female form. Article
near bottom
(use when Ritter (von) appears in the name of a person)
{{German title von}}
links talk edit 		In German personal names, von is a preposition which approximately means of or from and usually denotes some sort of nobility. While von (always lower case) is part of the family name or territorial designation, not a first or middle name, if the noble is referred to by his last name, use Schiller, Clausewitz or Goethe, not von Schiller, etc. Article
near bottom
(use when von (without further title) appears in the name of a person)
